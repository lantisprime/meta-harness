### Findings

**P3-1 — `executor.malformed-source` is a mild taxonomy downgrade for the escape-class species.**
- Evidence: `runtime.py:297` and `runtime.py:306` journal *all* preflight species — NUL/`SyntaxError` parse failures *and* forbidden-dunder / `__builtins__` refusals — under one kind. `_ast_preflight` (`runtime.py:27-33`) frames the dunder/`__builtins__` refusals as the `().__class__.__bases__[0].__subclasses__()` *escape class*. The codebase itself warns (`runtime.py:205-208`) that collapsing a security-relevant refusal into a broader kind "would record a mere read failure as an escape attempt, corrupting the evidence taxonomy for any consumer that buckets by kind."
- Failure scenario: a downstream evidence consumer that buckets by `kind` and treats `executor.malformed-source` as "input defect, non-security" under-weights a genuine sandbox-escape attempt, because the escape and the parse failure share a kind.
- Mitigants (why P3, not blocking): the exact species is fully preserved in the event `reason` (`runtime.py:461-469`), so full-fidelity-evidence and controlled-attribution invariants hold; the refusal is enforced; the change is strictly *additive* (prior state: zero journal events for dunder/`__builtins__` refusals — a *worse* attribution gap than any relabelling here); and `executor.path-escape` is host-boundary-specific (no existing source-namespace escape kind to map onto).
