# System boundaries and complementary functioning

The most consequential feature of the model is the **boundary between systems**. Information is processed, encoded, and recalled *separately* in each decision-making system. Human memory is **not a single reservoir** any process can access arbitrarily. Memories learned in one action-selection system **do not spontaneously transfer** to another (they can transfer only through experience and time-consuming consolidation).

Practical consequences of the boundaries:

- To perform a behavior with a given system's functionality, the learner must **access that system's memories, which requires having learned them with that system's unique learning mechanism.** You cannot get procedural fluency by deliberative explanation, or deliberative flexibility by drilling.
- Each system has unique strengths and unique limits: Deliberative = creative but effortful, variable, imprecise; Procedural = precise and integrated but inflexible; Instinctual = fast learning but only a limited innate repertoire.
- **To overcome one system's limitation requires support from another** — so many behaviors need multiple systems working simultaneously.

The systems are **not hierarchical** and there is no clear evidence any one monitors or regulates the others. They are complementary:

- All have fast and slow, conscious and unconscious components; none is purely rational or irrational, selfish or altruistic.
- **No system specializes in a task** (driving, music, math, conversation). The system in charge is whichever processes the *type of information* the situation demands. Driving a familiar route uses the same operations as playing a familiar song or solving a familiar problem.

A worked example — driving an *unfamiliar* route: the **Deliberative** System navigates, the **Procedural** System operates the wheel, the **Instinctual** System maintains proper arousal, and the **Perceptual** System filters extraneous information and encodes the world-state the others act on. One important interaction is the **feedback loop**: action-selection systems redirect the Perceptual System's attention, and the somatic states the Instinctual System produces (pounding heart, sweaty palms) feed back in as sensations that get re-categorized.
