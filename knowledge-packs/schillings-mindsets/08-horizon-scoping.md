# Horizon scoping — declare the window your reasoning is valid in

"My team's goal is to develop whatever technology will be needed to make
Gemini incredible **between one month and one year from now**. One month,
because working on what is needed in one week is a very different type of
job; one year, because I don't think anybody can really predict anything
that far."

The skill: before reasoning about the future, fix *both edges* of the
window explicitly.

- The **near edge** is a job-identity boundary, not a scheduling detail —
  week-scale work has different methods, staffing, and success criteria than
  month-scale work, and mixing them corrupts both.
- The **far edge** is an epistemic honesty boundary: past it, analysis is
  storytelling. Claiming a longer horizon than you can predict doesn't make
  planning strategic, it makes it fiction.
- Within the declared window, ambition is legitimate ("that's already pretty
  ambitious in my opinion") — the discipline is in refusing work that
  belongs to a different horizon, in either direction.

He applies the same clock to predictions in the talk ("I would predict that
in **one year** we'll let models generate the code and nobody will look at
it") — dated, checkable, inside his stated window of competence.
