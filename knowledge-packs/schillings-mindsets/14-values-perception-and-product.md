# Values — perceived responsiveness over benchmarks, product over technology

Two preference orderings from the BeOS Bible interview, visible as
choices between viable alternatives (the detection signature of the
values pillar):

**Responsiveness-as-perceived beats throughput-as-measured.** "Speed is
mostly a perception thing... BeOS gave a very snappy feel to the machine"
even where raw graphics throughput was lower; what he engineered for was
"always a thread ready to respond to a user interaction, even when
heavily loaded." Given the choice between winning the benchmark and
winning the user's felt experience, he structurally committed the whole
architecture (thread-per-window) to the latter. The value generalizes:
measure the quantity the constraint-holder actually experiences, and be
willing to lose the public metric.

**Product beats technology.** "It's very dangerous... to start to be
fascinated by technology and become a technology company instead of
being a product company." Said by the person *most* fascinated by the
technology — he then acted on it: "I just gave away my last piece of
operating system work... I want to write apps and stuff that really
makes the product useful for users." The ranking (user-visible value >
technical depth he personally loves) is credible precisely because it
cost him the work he enjoyed most.

Consumption note: these are `EvaluationCriterion` material — anchors for
judging whether a specialist ranks options the way Schillings would
(e.g. prefer the design that keeps interaction latency bounded over the
one that wins throughput benchmarks).
